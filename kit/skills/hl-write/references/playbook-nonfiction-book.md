# Non-Fiction Book Playbook — Book · Proposal

Structurally closer to academic writing in evidence rigor, but paced like fiction at book length. Chapter is the unit in both the finished book and its earlier proposal stage.

**Route note:** when a request just says "a book" without specifying, confirm at Recon whether the deliverable is the proposal or the manuscript itself — the two variants below carry different skeletons, evidence, and unit definitions.

## Track

The finished book always defaults to the long-form track — chapters exist from the outline stage, so the persistent workspace applies regardless of final word count. The proposal stage defaults to short-form; if sample chapters push it past the long-form threshold, confirm the track at Recon rather than assuming.

## Sub-genre Note: Narrative vs. Prescriptive

Memoir and narrative non-fiction carry the highest anecdote-fabrication risk in this genre — every scene presented as remembered fact must survive the anecdote-labeling rule below. Prescriptive/how-to non-fiction (business, self-help, health) shifts the primary risk to invented statistics and case-study details rather than personal-memory anecdotes — apply the same sourced-or-labeled standard to case studies that narrative non-fiction applies to personal anecdotes.

## Variant: Non-Fiction Book

**Skeleton:** Front matter (TOC signals the argument's flow) → Intro (thesis/promise) → Body chapters, each: hook → claim → evidence/anecdote → bridge to the next chapter → Conclusion (synthesis + call to action).

**Front matter check:** confirm the TOC's chapter titles read as a coherent argument on their own before drafting begins — a reader skimming just the TOC should be able to follow the thesis.

**Mandatory evidence:** sourced claims and statistics; anecdotes either independently verifiable or explicitly labeled illustrative/composite.

> **Required — anecdote-labeling:** an anecdote presented as a real, verified event needs a source; anything reconstructed, composited, or hypothetical must be labeled as such in the text (e.g. "Consider a founder who…") — never blur the line between reported fact and illustrative device.

**Fabrication risks:** invented statistics; fabricated real-person quotes or anecdotes presented as verified fact (the dominant risk in memoir/narrative non-fiction); fake comp titles carried over from the proposal into the finished book's back matter.

**Review criteria:**
1. Each chapter's claim ties back to the book's central thesis/TOC — no orphan chapters
2. Claims are sourced, or anecdotes are explicitly labeled
3. Chapter opens with a hook and closes with a bridge to the next
4. Voice/register stays consistent across chapters

**Length:** typically 60,000–90,000 words.

**Unit:** one Build unit = one chapter.

## Variant: Proposal Stage

**Skeleton:** Overview → Market/Comp titles → Author platform → Chapter-by-chapter outline (1–3 paragraphs per chapter) → 1–3 sample chapters.

**Mandatory evidence:** comp titles that are real, verifiable published books; market claims sourced, not asserted; sample chapters held to the same anecdote-labeling rule as the finished book.

**Author platform evidence:** platform claims (newsletter subscribers, social following, speaking engagements, media mentions) need real numbers as of a stated date — "a large following" is not evidence, "40,000 newsletter subscribers as of March 2026" is.

**Fabrication risks:** fake comp titles; overstated market/audience-size claims with no sourcing; sample chapters that promise a structure the chapter-by-chapter outline doesn't actually deliver.

**Review criteria:**
1. Comp titles are real and genuinely comparable, not name-dropped for prestige
2. Market claims are sourced
3. Chapter-by-chapter outline matches the thesis stated in Overview
4. Sample chapters represent the book's actual voice and structure, not a polished outlier

**Length:** 10–25 double-spaced pages; up to ~50 with sample chapters included.

**Unit:** one Build unit = one outline chapter entry, or one sample chapter during the sample-chapter pass.

## Anti-Fabrication Guardrail (both variants)

> **Required — toc-thesis-linkage:** every chapter's claim traces back to the book's stated central thesis before Ship; a chapter that doesn't tie back is cut, folded into another chapter, or the thesis is revised — never left as an unexplained tangent. Statistics and quotes follow the same cite-before-claim rule as every other citation-bearing playbook.
