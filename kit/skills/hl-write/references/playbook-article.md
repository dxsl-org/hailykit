# Article Playbook — News Article · Blog Post

Two short-form, attribution-driven variants, both audience-first. They differ in ordering discipline: strict inverted-pyramid for news, search-intent-structured for blog.

## Track

Both variants default to the short-form track — well under the ~8,000-word long-form threshold. A multi-part investigative series is the one exception: treat each part as its own unit and confirm the track at Recon if the series as a whole grows past the threshold.

## Variant: News Article

**Skeleton:** Lede (5W1H, ≤30 words) → Nut Graf (stakes/significance) → supporting facts in descending order of importance → attribution/quotes → optional background/context tail.

*Example lede:* "Da Nang's city council voted 7-2 Tuesday to ban single-use plastics at public markets starting January, following two years of pilot programs." — who/what/when/where in one sentence, why implied, ≤30 words.

**Mandatory evidence:** named, attributable sources; verifiable quotes; dated facts.

**Developing stories:** when new facts arrive after publish, write the addition as a new unit with an explicit "Updated:" timestamp — never silently overwrite facts already attributed to a source.

**Correction transparency:** if a published fact turns out to be wrong (not just superseded by new information), add a visible correction note rather than silently editing the original text.

**Fabrication risks:** invented quotes or sources; unverified statistics stated as fact; a headline that oversells or contradicts the body (clickbait mismatch).

**Review criteria:**
1. Lede answers all of 5W1H in ≤30 words
2. Body runs in strict descending-importance order, not chronological order
3. Every quote and source is attributable
4. Headline matches the body's actual claim

**Length:** straight news 400–800 words; feature/longform runs 1,000–3,000+ words.

**Unit:** one Build unit = the lede+nut-graf opening, or one subsequent block of descending-importance facts. Most straight-news pieces are short enough to close as a single unit.

## Variant: Blog Post

**Skeleton:** Hook → thesis/promise → H2-structured body sections answering the target search intent → conclusion/CTA. Same attribution discipline as news.

*Example H2 outline for "best budget espresso machines":* Why budget doesn't mean bad → Top 5 picks → What to check before buying → FAQs — each H2 maps to a distinct search-query variant, not a clever subheading.

**Mandatory evidence:** named/attributable sources for factual claims; verifiable statistics; dated facts.

**Search intent:** confirm the target keyword/search intent during Recon — H2s are named to match query patterns readers actually search, not clever wordplay.

**Fabrication risks:** unverified statistics stated as fact; invented expert quotes; a title that promises content the body doesn't deliver.

**Review criteria:**
1. Hook states the reader's payoff in the first 1–2 sentences
2. Each H2 section answers a distinct piece of the declared search intent
3. Claims and sources are attributable
4. Conclusion delivers a clear call to action
5. Title matches body content

**Length:** SEO blog 1,500–2,500 words; competitive topics commonly run 2,500–4,000+ words — treat these as working targets set by the brief's stated intent, not fixed counts.

**Evergreen updates:** refreshing an old post resets its "Updated:" date but keeps the original URL/slug — treat the refresh as a new unit layered onto the existing draft, not a full rewrite, unless the thesis itself changed.

**Unit:** one Build unit = one H2 section.

## Anti-Fabrication Guardrail (both variants)

> **Required — attribution-before-print:** no quote, statistic, or named source reaches the draft without a matching entry in `research/`. Unsourced claims are flagged for the writer to source or hedge, never silently invented.
